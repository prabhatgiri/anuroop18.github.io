Electric Propulsion

Electric propulsion: The future of aerospace propulsion.

Today, hundreds of electric thruster powered satellites are operating in space. Ion and Hall thrusters have been successfully used for propulsion in deep-space scientific mission. So this isn't some technology that will come in future, its here, working right now. To understand how electric thrusters work, you'll need basic knowledge of Plasma Physics, Ion accelerators, cathodes, Electromagnotism, Fluid Dynamics and many other techniques. Electric Thrusters of today provide very low thrust ( <5N ), the challenge is to increase the thrust and life time of the engine.

Various methods are used for propulsion of rockets, spacecrafts and other aerospace vehicles. Our of them, electrical methods are most elegant. But until now, there have not been enough advancements in electric propulsion technology, so that they can be used to propel any aerospace vehicle. For now their use is limited to providing very small thrusts to satellites in space.
